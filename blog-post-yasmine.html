Yasmine's Story: #ITriedTherapy

Was this your first time trying a session of therapy? Was it what you expected? What'd you think?

This was my first time trying a session of therapy and I will say I was a little nervous going into it. I didn't really know what to expect, but when it comes down to it, I can confidently say I left my first session with a sense of peace that I couldn't have prepared myself for. I had expected myself to be tongue-tied and not to know what subjects or experiences to bring up. Instead, my thoughts and feelings spilled out in a way that made this therapy session enlightening in a way I had never known. It required some vulnerability on my end, and it was tense facing these feelings, but what started as a one-time try proved to me how important and helpful therapy can be, especially when it brings to light what I didn't even know I was struggling with, and with it brings a sense of calm.

Before you went, did you struggle with “what am I going to tell this stranger?” What would you tell yourself about your experience?

I definitely struggled with the "stranger" aspect of things when I first booked my appointment. However, I felt a kind of comfort knowing that this stranger had a similar cultural background to me, which then helped me open up more easily. If I were to tell myself something about this experience, I would say, "Brace yourself. This is not what you expected it to be. It's not preventative, or reactionary, or a solution - but a way to help you battle some roadblocks along the way to becoming a better you."
